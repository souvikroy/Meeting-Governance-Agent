# Governance Policies (plain-English)

These are written the way a non-technical compliance lead would hand them over.
The agent operates on the **meaning**, not on keywords or regexes.

## Policy 1 — Compensation talk is off the record
If people start talking about anyone's salary, bonus, equity, severance, or pay
package, do **not** keep any recording of that part. We don't want a copy of it
anywhere — not a redacted one, not a "muted" one. It should be like it was never
said. (Even a passing, figure-free reference to compensation as a topic — e.g.
mentioning "comp philosophy" or that pay will be discussed later — is a borderline
case that must NOT be silently kept: treat it as genuinely uncertain, and escalate
it for human review rather than committing it.)

## Policy 2 — Never record the deal codename
This transaction is internally referred to as **"Project Atlas."** Any moment
where someone says the codename in a way that ties it to *this deal* must not be
retained at all. The codename is need-to-know and must never end up in a stored
recording. (Note: an unrelated use of the same words — e.g. a movie title that
merely contains the phrase and is not tied to this transaction — is not covered.)

## Policy 3 — Flag possible legal exposure, but keep it
If someone seems to admit to something that could be a legal or regulatory
problem — they broke a contract, they knew about a defect, they missed a required
filing, that kind of thing — we DO want that recorded, but it must be **flagged
so our legal team reviews it**. Keep it; just raise a hand on it.

## Policy 4 — Hide financial account and card numbers
If anyone reads out a bank account number, a credit/debit card number, or similar
sensitive financial identifiers, keep the recording but **black out the number
itself**. We want the conversation, not the digits. (A phone number, an address,
or other non-financial identifiers are NOT covered by this policy.)

## Policy 5 (standing rule) — Consent to record
We only ever record people who have agreed to be recorded. If someone on the call
has **not** consented, nothing they say is kept — **full stop** — regardless of
what it's about. Consent is a **hard gate**: if a speaker hasn't consented, no
other policy can cause their speech to be kept, flagged, or redacted-and-kept.
Non-consent always wins. (Enforced before any transcription or inference occurs.)
